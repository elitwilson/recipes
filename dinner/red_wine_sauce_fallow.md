## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount | Notes |
|------------|--------|-------|
| Red wine | 500ml | Optionally 375ml wine + 125ml port |
| Shallots | 2-3 | Finely minced |
| Tomato paste | 1-2 tbsp | Adds body and helps emulsification |
| Beef stock reduction cubes | 2 | |
| Fresh parsley | To taste | Chopped |
| Tarragon | Small bunch | |
| Cold butter | 25g | Cubed |

---

## Instructions

1. **SautÃ© shallots:** In a heavy-based pan, melt a small knob of butter and gently sautÃ© the minced shallots until soft and translucent (about 3-4 minutes).
2. **Bloom tomato paste:** Add tomato paste and cook for about 1 minute until it darkens slightly. This removes the raw taste and builds depth.
3. **Reduce wine:** Deglaze with wine (and port if using). Bring to a boil and reduce by half. You're looking for it to coat the back of a spoon.
4. **Add stock:** Add 2 beef stock reduction cubes. Whisk until melted and fully incorporated. Let it simmer for 2-3 minutes.
5. **Finish:** Add the fresh parsley and tarragon, followed by the cold butter. Whisk constantly to emulsify the butter into the sauce.
6. **Infuse and strain:** Let the herbs infuse for 1-2 minutes, then strain through a fine-mesh sieve.
7. **Serve:** Serve immediately with your beef wellington.

---

## Notes

**Make Ahead:**
- Prepare through step 4 (reduce wine, add stock cubes)
- Cool and refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 3 days
- Day of serving: Reheat gently over low heat, then add the fresh herbs and whisk in the cold butter just before serving

**Why tomato paste:**
- Adds body, umami, and natural pectin that helps the sauce hold together
- Pure wine reductions can be thin and one-dimensional; tomato paste gives it structure
